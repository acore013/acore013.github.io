Amy Coreas

Biology Student 🩺

I am currently a 4th year biology major pursuing healthcare, more specifically a career as a first assist surgical tech. Most of my time these past 4 years has been spent taking a variety of STEM courses such as math, biology, chemistry, physics, ecology, and environmental science, to name a few. Because of this, a lot of my time is spent doing lab reports, projects, carrying out lab experiments, or studying for exams. Due to how rigorous these courses can be, things get really stressful sometimes, especially during busy parts of the quarter when everything seems to pile up at once. However, because of this I have learned how to manage my time more efficiently and stay organized even during stressful situations. Not to mention, I have also become much more confident in myself and my abilities as a student.

Although my main interest is in healthcare, I have also really enjoyed taking classes like herpetology and entomology because they allowed me to explore completely different sides of biology. In entomology, I worked on insect collections, identification projects, and field observations, which helped me become more detail-oriented and observant. Herpetology also gave me a better understanding of reptiles, amphibians, biodiversity, and ecosystems. I liked that these classes were more hands on and involved going outside the classroom instead of only sitting through lectures. A lot of my other science courses have also involved laboratory experiments, scientific writing, and data analysis. Honestly, a lot of labs and projects did not always go perfectly the first time, so I had to learn how to work through mistakes and figure out what went wrong instead of immediately getting frustrated. Experiences like that taught me how important it is to be patient with myself and keep trying even when things become overwhelming.

Balancing multiple science courses and labs at once has taught me how to manage my time better and work under pressure without giving up. I enjoy learning new things and challenging myself, especially in subjects related to biology and healthcare. I also enjoy working with other people and being in environments where communication and collaboration are important. I mainly made this website to showcase some of the classes, projects, lab work, and experiences I have had throughout college so far as a biology student. As I continue through school, I hope to keep gaining more hands on experience and continue working toward a future career in the medical field.
